## PART 2: WHAT WE CAN LEARN & OUR CLASSIFICATION

### A. Key Learnings from Duckworth

**Price:**
1. **$130–$175 is a real zone for premium merino lifestyle pieces.** Duckworth proves customers pay $145–$169 for Tunnel Hoods and ¼ Zips. If our brand has a lifestyle/outerwear-adjacent ambition, this is the price ceiling we can credibly aim for.
2. **Men's should price $20–$30 above women's for the same category.** Duckworth's men's median ($165) vs women's ($145) reflects real market expectations — men's outerwear commands a premium.
3. **The underwear 4-pack at $176 is a smart bundling move.** Instead of pricing individual briefs at $49, Duckworth bundles 4 for $176 — more compelling value perception at higher AoV.

**Category:**
1. **Outerwear/mid-layer is the revenue engine, not base layers.** Duckworth's avg Outerwear price ($174–$252) dwarfs base layers. A brand with the right positioning can lead with hoods and ¼ zips rather than thermal underwear.
2. **Fewer styles, fully committed, beats many styles half-committed.** 95 styles vs Minus33's 176, but Duckworth's 29 avg SKUs/style in Outerwear means they never launch a product without full investment. New brands should copy this discipline.
3. **Women's Tunnel Hood as a hero SKU is a genuine differentiator.** No other merino brand leads with a Tunnel Hood. Finding your own signature piece is more powerful than copying the standard top/bottom/underwear playbook.

**Color:**
1. **35 colors beats 85.** Duckworth proves a premium brand can own a tight, nature-coded palette and go deeper per color. Spruce + Midnight as twin anchors is a master class in palette discipline.
2. **Don't gender-code the palette differently.** Both genders get the same nature palette — this simplifies production and reinforces brand identity. Contrast with Minus33's split (men's outdoor/camo vs women's lifestyle colors).
3. **Earth tones code as premium.** Spruce, Auburn, Natural, Coyote, Cedar — these colors signal quality and Montana/outdoor heritage. For a lifestyle-positioned merino brand, ditch the bright statements.

**Size:**
1. **Flat XS–XL for women's with NO plus is a deliberate premium positioning choice.** Duckworth accepts losing plus-size customers to maintain fit integrity at this price point. A new brand should choose the same path intentionally, not accidentally.
2. **S–XXL flat for men's is the right pattern** — same as Minus33 but stopping at XXL rather than extending to 6XL. This is the defensible range for a premium brand.
3. **No numeric sizing at all** — Duckworth doesn't do waist/inseam sizing for pants, only XS–XL. This keeps production simple.

### C. Price Positioning Recommendation

**Relative to Duckworth:** Position 15–25% below Duckworth on equivalent pieces.
- Duckworth's Tunnel Hood: $169 → we target $139–$149
- Duckworth's ¼ Zip: $165 → we target $125–$139
- Duckworth's Long Sleeve Crew: $145 → we target $99–$119
- Duckworth's underwear pack: $176/4-pack → we do $45–$49 individual (don't bundle at launch)

**The rational:** Duckworth earns its premium through brand heritage (Montana, 100+ years of ranching/wool sourcing). A newer brand can't command the same premium yet, but can target the $99–$149 zone that Duckworth's customers consider accessible.

**Priority tiers:**
1. **Core ($85–$119):** Tops and bottoms. Volume drivers.
2. **Elevated ($125–$159):** ¼ Zip + Tunnel Hood. Brand signature pieces.
3. **Entry ($38–$52):** Underwear. Repeat purchase and trial.
