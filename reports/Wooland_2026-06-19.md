## PART 2: WHAT WE CAN LEARN & OUR CLASSIFICATION

### A. Key Learnings from Wooland

**Price:**
1. The $80–$99 Core tier is where tops live — this is a high-volume, lower-margin category that builds brand loyalty and drives repeat purchase. A new brand should anchor their tee/tank line here.
2. Dresses command $140–$179 with strong SKU support — consumers in this segment accept premium pricing for dresses in quality fabrics. Don't undercut here; price dresses at $138–$168 to signal quality.
3. The OPP ($10–$59) underwear/bra line is a smart customer acquisition tool. Entry-level price points bring new customers into the brand ecosystem. Consider launching 3–5 underwear SKUs as brand introducers.

**Category:**
1. Dress-first strategy: Building Wooland's brand identity around dresses (33% of SKUs, avg $153) creates a halo that lifts the entire range. If you want to be known for something, lead with dresses — they photograph well, are gifted frequently, and have strong AOV.
2. The Tee + Dress two-pillar model works: Tees generate traffic at lower price points while dresses drive revenue. A new brand should replicate this dual-engine model rather than trying to be all things at once.
3. Men's is not a priority for Wooland and that's fine. Unless there is a specific men's customer target, a new brand should go women's-first and add men's only after proving product-market fit.

**Color:**
1. Black is non-negotiable — run it in everything from launch. 21.5% of all SKUs are Black; it's the single best-selling color by 3× margin.
2. A single signature seasonal color (Wooland's is Marine Blue) gives the brand a recognizable color DNA beyond neutrals. Pick one and run it consistently across categories.
3. Earth tones (Olive, Espresso) bridge neutral and statement — they're safer than fully seasonal colors but more interesting than pure basics. A 10–15% earth tone mix is a good starting point.
4. Statement colors (Marionberry, Verde) function as excitement/newness — reserve them for mid-season drops rather than launch. Don't over-index on statements at launch.

**Size:**
1. Full straight-size run (XS–XL) from day one — do not launch with S/M/L only; it signals an underinvested brand.
2. Plus sizing (1X–3X) is an investment Wooland makes at scale — if targeting an inclusive positioning, plan for it from the design stage (grading, fit models). Do not tack it on after launch.
3. XS is slightly deprioritized even by Wooland — acceptable to run lighter on XS at launch.
4. Men's numeric sizing (waist) is low priority unless specifically targeting denim.

### C. Price Positioning Recommendation

**Positioning: Alongside Wooland, not below**

Wooland prices tees at $78–$98 and dresses at $138–$178. Do not undercut this — it signals lower quality and erodes margin. A new brand should:

| Tier | Recommended Range | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Entry / OPP | $28–$68 | Underwear, bras, bike shorts — match Wooland's Roam line |
| Core (Tops) | $78–$98 | Match Wooland's tee/tank core exactly |
| Mid (Leggings, Skirts) | $118–$148 | Slight premium over Wooland to signal quality |
| Elevated (Dresses, Knitwear) | $138–$178 | Align with Wooland's best-selling dress tier |
| Premium | $188–$228 | Pants, blazers — only if fabric/construction justifies |

**Key price points to hit at launch:**
- $88 tee (Core anchor)
- $98 tank (Core anchor)
- $148 dress (Volume/revenue driver)
- $168 dress (Elevated flagship)
