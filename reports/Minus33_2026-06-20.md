## PART 2: WHAT WE CAN LEARN & OUR CLASSIFICATION

### A. Key Learnings from Minus33

**Price:**
1. **The $70–$90 Core is validated for merino base layers.** Both men's and women's peak at $89.99 median. This is the sweet spot for thermal/midweight merino and where competition centers. Any brand entering this space should anchor here.
2. **Socks as a volume/traffic driver is a real strategy.** 30.5% of SKUs at $18–$25 per pair creates a very low entry price point and repeat purchase behavior. This is a separate business unit running inside the brand.
3. **The valley at $30–$50 is structural.** It exists because $30–$50 is too high for accessories and too low for merino apparel. A new brand should price underwear at $35–$50 and T-shirts at $60–$70 minimum — don't get stuck in the valley.

**Category:**
1. **Tops first, bottoms second.** The Tops category is always the hero (63% of Women's apparel SKUs, 65% of Men's). A new brand should over-index on tops before building out bottoms.
2. **Underwear punches above its weight.** Only 11 styles but highest avg SKU depth (19.9). Underwear builds repeat customers and is easy to replenish. Prioritize it early even with a small style count.
3. **Minus33 neglects accessories depth.** Headwear averages only 7.7 SKUs/style and scarves 5.0 — these are clearly not the brand's focus. An opportunity exists for a brand to own merino accessories more deeply.

**Color:**
1. **Lead with Black.** Across both genders, Black is 2–3× the next color and should be the anchor in any new launch. A new brand can safely open with 60–70% neutrals.
2. **Men's outdoor palette vs. Women's lifestyle palette is a real split.** Men's does Olive Drab, Tan, Forest Green; women's does Burgundy, Cream, Slate Blue. Don't use one palette for both genders.
3. **85 colors is a complexity trap to avoid at launch.** Minus33's long tail of 85 colors creates operational overhead. A new brand should start with 6–10 colors per style maximum and expand based on sell-through.

**Size:**
1. **Flat S–XL curve is the right investment model.** Equal SKU count across S/M/L/XL signals no deprioritization of any core size. A new brand should open with equal depth across S–XL before adding XS/2XL.
2. **XS matters for women's — invest equally.** Minus33's women's XS (91 SKUs) nearly matches S–XL. Cutting XS is a mistake in women's merino where the customer skews smaller.
3. **Don't open with 6XL.** Minus33's 4XL–6XL have 2–8 SKUs total — basically token. A new brand can safely stop at 2XL/3XL and revisit once core sizes are established.

### C. Price Positioning Recommendation

**Relative to Minus33:** Position alongside or 5–10% above on core apparel.
- Minus33's Core sits at $69.99–$89.99. Price our T-shirts at $70–$75 and thermal layers at $89–$99 to be perceived as competitive-to-premium vs. Minus33 without extreme differentiation.
- Minus33's underwear at $35–$50 is already reasonable — match it; do not undercut.
- Avoid the $30–$50 vacuum for apparel. If pricing a T-shirt, start at $65 minimum.

**Priority tiers for launch:**
1. **Core ($65–$95):** Launch here. T-shirts, crews, thermals. Where the market lives.
2. **Entry ($35–$50):** Underwear only. Drives trial and repeat purchase.
3. **Elevated ($105–$115):** One ¼ zip per gender. Signals range authority without overextending.

**Specific price targets to hit:**
- Lightweight T-shirt: $70 (aligns with Minus33's $70.21 avg for this style)
- Midweight crew: $89.99 (exact market anchor price)
- Midweight ¼ zip: $109.99–$112.99
- Thermal bottoms: $89.99
- Boxer brief / underwear: $44.99
